Now let's try an example where we have a shift of a different number. Say, 3.

This means each letter in our plaintext would be shifted forward 3 times. For example, "A" would become "D". And "ABC" would become "DEF".



Now let's say the secret message you want to encrypt is... "B", with a caesar shift of 8. To get the secret message, we will shift each letter by eight places. "B" is the second letter of the alphabet, plus 8 equals the tenth letter of the alphabet so "B" becomes "J"
